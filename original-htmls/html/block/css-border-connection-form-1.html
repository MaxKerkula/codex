Border Connection Forms

The -ah-border-connection-form property specifies the shape of connections between borders. This property controls how borders meet at intersections and is available in AH Formatter V6.6 and later. The property accepts three values: mixed, wedge, and precedence, each creating different visual effects at border intersections.
Mixed Connection Form

When -ah-border-connection-form is mixed, T-intersections of borders with the same thickness connect with squared off ends. Other connections are wedge-shaped.

Wedge Connection Form

When -ah-border-connection-form is wedge, all borders connect with a wedge shape. Connections form an X shape at cross-shaped intersections, and form a Y shape at T-intersections.

Precedence Connection Form

When -ah-border-connection-form is precedence, the thicker border is drawn to the edge. When intersecting borders have the same thickness, the border with the higher border priorityRefer to 17.6.2.1 Border conflict resolution for details of border priority. is drawn to the edge.

Border Connection Forms on Block Elements

For <div> elements, when mixed or wedge is specified, all border intersections are wedge-shaped, but when precedence is specified, the shape depends on the relative priority of the borders.
