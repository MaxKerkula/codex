Hyphenation processing of words that consist of uppercase letters
You can specify whether to hyphenate uppercase words with -ah-hyphenate-caps-word. The setting is effective when hyphens: true; is specified. Even small-caps processed characters are considered lowercase if the original characters are lowercase. The setting of -ah-hyphenate-caps-word: false-all; is available from AH Formatter V7.2.
If you specify -ah-hyphenate-caps-word: true; (default), normal Hyphenation is preformed. Words are hyphenated regardless of whether they are uppercase or lowercase.

If you specify -ah-hyphenate-caps-word: false;, words that are all uppercase will not be hyphenated. Words that are not all uppercase, such as words that have only the first letter capitalized, are hyphenated.

A string like ANTENNA-HOUSE with a hyphen is considered multiple words. Therefore, there is a possibility of line breaking at the hyphen position.
